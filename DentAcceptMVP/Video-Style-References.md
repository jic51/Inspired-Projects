## REFERENCIA 01 — DENTADURA COMPLETA
**Archivo:** `REF-01-dentadura-completa.png`  
**Cuándo se usa:** Escena 1 de TODOS los videos (apertura), Escena 7 de TODOS los videos (cierre)

### Prompt canónico (copia exacto en Gemini / Flow)
```
Medical illustration, full frontal view of a complete healthy adult 
human dentition — both upper and lower dental arches visible simultaneously.

PERSPECTIVE: Camera looking straight-on at the front of the closed mouth. 
The teeth are gently together (not biting hard). The lips are NOT visible — 
the view begins at the gum line. Slight downward tilt of 5 degrees so we 
see just a hint of the upper occlusal surfaces.

UPPER ARCH: 16 teeth total visible (or partially visible). The 2 central 
incisors dominate the center — broad, flat labial surfaces, slightly convex. 
Moving outward: lateral incisors (narrower), canines (slightly pointed tips), 
premolars, and molars tapering toward the back corners of the frame.

LOWER ARCH: 16 teeth total, slightly smaller and more compact. Lower central 
incisors are the smallest teeth. Lower arch follows the natural curve — wider 
toward the back.

TEETH COLOR: Warm ivory-white (#F5F0E8). NOT pure white, NOT yellow. 
Subtle gradient from slightly more opaque at the gingival third to slightly 
more translucent at the incisal edge. Matte-satin surface — no harsh 
specular highlights, no mirror-like reflections.

GUMS: Firm, healthy salmon-pink (#E8A090). Continuous scalloped margin 
around every tooth. Gentle papilla peaks between each tooth. Subtle 
stippling texture (orange-peel effect). No inflammation, no recession, 
no pockets. Upper gum fills the top 25% of the image. Lower gum fills 
the bottom 20%.

BACKGROUND: Warm off-white (#FAF9F6). No gradient, no shadows on background.
A very subtle drop shadow beneath the lower gum to give depth.

STYLE: Medical illustration, NOT photorealistic, NOT cartoon, NOT 3D render. 
Hand-drawn quality with clean linework. Similar to ADA patient education 
illustrations. Think Netter's Atlas aesthetic applied to dental patient education.

FORMAT: Horizontal 16:9, 1920×1080px.
LIGHTING: Soft studio light from top-left 45 degrees. No harsh shadows.
NO text, NO labels, NO arrows, NO watermarks, NO background elements.
```

### Constantes visuales (para verificar antes de aprobar)
| Elemento | Valor correcto | Error común |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Fondo | #FAF9F6 warm off-white | Gris o blanco puro |
| Dientes | Ivory #F5F0E8 | Demasiado blancos o amarillos |
| Encía superior | Salmon #E8A090 | Rojo brillante o muy pálido |
| Estilo | Medical illustration | Fotorrealista o cartoon |
| Ángulo | Frontal, boca cerrada | Vista interior, vista lateral |
| Texto | NINGUNO | Nunca debe haber labels |

### NO debe incluir
- ❌ Sección transversal (cross-section) o vista interior
- ❌ Raíces, hueso, pulpa — solo coronas visibles
- ❌ Labios, nariz, cara completa
- ❌ Placa, caries, manchas (esta imagen = boca PERFECTA)
- ❌ Fondo con gradiente o textura
- ❌ Diente torcido, girado, separación excesiva entre dientes

## REFERENCIA 02 — CLOSE-UP MOLARES (VISTA BUCCAL)
**Archivo:** `REF-02-close-up-molares.png`  
**Cuándo se usa:** Escena 3 de: Crown, Root Canal, Implant, Extraction, Filling, Bridge, Deep Cleaning

### Prompt canónico
```
Medical illustration, exterior buccal close-up view of the lower right 
posterior dental arch. Camera looking at the outer (cheek-side) surface 
of three teeth from a slightly elevated angle (15 degrees above horizontal).

COMPOSITION — 3 FULL TEETH + 2 PARTIAL:
- Far left: partial view of lower right second premolar (half visible, 
  cut off by left frame edge)
- Left-center: lower right FIRST PREMOLAR — full tooth, healthy, complete
- CENTER (PROTAGONIST): lower right FIRST MOLAR — the largest, most 
  detailed tooth in the frame. Broad buccal surface, two visible buccal 
  cusps with the central groove between them. This is the focal tooth.
- Right-center: lower right SECOND MOLAR — full tooth, slightly smaller 
  than the first molar
- Far right: partial view of lower right third molar (barely visible, 
  cut off by right frame edge)

ALIGNMENT — CRITICAL: All five teeth share the same vertical long axis 
direction — perfectly upright, no rotation or tilt. The buccal surfaces 
face directly toward the camera. 85% of each tooth shows the flat buccal 
face; only 15% shows the occlusal edge at the top. Zero rotation on any tooth.

GINGIVA: Firm salmon-pink, scalloped margin. The gum line runs across 
the bottom third of the frame in a gentle upward curve from left to right 
(following the jaw arch). Subtle papilla between each tooth pair.

TEETH: Warm ivory-white #F5F0E8, fully opaque, satin finish. 
The first molar cusp tips have very subtle groove details.

BACKGROUND: Warm off-white #FAF9F6. Soft studio light from upper-left.
Medical illustration style. Horizontal 16:9.
NO cross-section, NO transparency, NO roots visible, NO text.
```

### NO debe incluir
- ❌ Diente central girado o torcido respecto a sus vecinos
- ❌ Vista oclusal (desde arriba) — solo buccal
- ❌ Cross-section o transparencia
- ❌ Dientes superiores o antagonistas

## REFERENCIA 03 — CLOSE-UP INCISIVOS (VISTA FRONTAL-LABIAL)
**Archivo:** `REF-03-close-up-incisivos.png`  
**Cuándo se usa:** Escena 3 de: Veneer, Whitening, Invisalign (resultado)

### Prompt canónico
```
Medical illustration, frontal labial close-up view of the upper anterior 
dental arch segment. Looking directly at the front (labial) surfaces. 
NO cross-section, NO internal anatomy, NO transparency.

COMPOSITION — 5 UNITS (left to right as viewer sees it):
1. LEFT EDGE: Right half of the upper right lateral incisor, cut off 
   by the left frame edge.
2. FULL TOOTH: Upper right central incisor — complete, healthy, upright. 
   Same width as the protagonist but slightly less detailed.
3. CENTER PROTAGONIST: Upper LEFT central incisor — main subject. 
   Broad flat labial surface, slightly convex, squared incisal edge 
   with subtly rounded corners. The most detailed and centered tooth.
4. FULL TOOTH: Upper left lateral incisor — noticeably narrower and 
   shorter than the centrals. Complete, healthy.
5. RIGHT EDGE: Left half of the upper left canine, cut off by the 
   right frame edge. Slightly pointed tip visible.

SIZE HIERARCHY: Both central incisors widest (8-9mm appearance). 
Lateral incisor narrower (6-7mm). Canine sliver barely visible.

ALIGNMENT: All teeth share same vertical axis. Incisal edges follow 
natural smile arc — centrals lowest, laterals slightly higher. 
Zero rotation or tilt. Only flat labial surface visible — no occlusal.

GINGIVA: Firm salmon-pink, scalloped. Gum line at 30% from bottom. 
Papilla peaks between each pair.

TEETH: Ivory-white #F5F0E8, fully opaque, satin. Natural translucency 
ONLY at the very incisal tip edge. NOT glassy or mirror-like.

BACKGROUND: Warm off-white #FAF9F6. Soft studio light upper-left 45°.
Medical illustration style. Horizontal 16:9.
```

### NO debe incluir
- ❌ Dos incisivos centrales completamente iguales como protagonistas — UNO es el foco
- ❌ Vista oclusal — solo labial
- ❌ Dientes inferiores

## REFERENCIA 04 — CROSS-SECTION MOLAR (SANO)
**Archivo:** `REF-04-crosssection-molar-sano.png`  
**Cuándo se usa:** Punto de partida para Crown, Root Canal, Filling, Implant (pre-tratamiento)

### Prompt canónico
```
Medical illustration, precise anatomical cross-section of a single 
lower first molar, cut along the mesiodistal (front-to-back) plane.

EXTERNAL SHAPE: The crown is broad with two visible cusps (mesial and 
distal buccal). The roots split into two distinct roots: mesial root 
(slightly angled forward) and distal root (slightly angled backward).

LAYERS (from outside in, color-coded but still looking like illustration):
1. ENAMEL — outer shell of the crown, 2-3mm thick, light blue-grey tint 
   (#D4E4F0), semi-translucent appearance at tips
2. DENTIN — main body under enamel and cementum, warm yellow-ivory 
   (#E8D5A3), makes up most of the tooth volume
3. PULP CHAMBER — centered in the crown, teardrop shape, soft pink-red 
   (#E8A090), extends down into two ROOT CANALS (one per root)
4. CEMENTUM — thin grey layer covering the roots (#B8B8A8)
5. PERIODONTAL LIGAMENT — very thin pale yellow line between cementum 
   and bone (#F0E8C8)
6. ALVEOLAR BONE — surrounds the roots, medium grey-beige (#C8B89A), 
   trabecular texture visible

THE TOOTH IS HEALTHY: No caries, no darkening, no infection, no cracks.
Pulp is uniformly pink. All layers intact and well-defined.

BACKGROUND: Warm off-white #FAF9F6 on each side of the cross-section.
Cut plane has a clean, flat edge with a thin dark outline.
Medical illustration style. Vertical orientation (portrait-ish) 
or centered in a 16:9 horizontal frame with ample white space on sides.
Anatomical labels are NOT shown — illustration only.
```

### NO debe incluir
- ❌ Caries, oscurecimiento, infección (esto es el ANTES-sano)
- ❌ Corona protésica — el diente es natural
- ❌ Implante, tornillo, material de relleno

## REFERENCIA 05 — TITLE CARD
**Archivo:** `REF-05-title-card-template.png`  
**Cuándo se usa:** Primera escena de TODOS los videos

### Especificaciones (crear en Canva / CapCut)
```
FONDO: Dark forest green sólido — #1a472a
LÍNEA DECORATIVA: 1px, color dorado-arena #C9A96E, 
                  ancho 60% del frame, centrada horizontalmente,
                  debajo del título principal

TÍTULO: [NOMBRE DEL PROCEDIMIENTO EN INGLÉS]
  - Fuente: Serif elegante (Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, o similar)
  - Tamaño: ~120px
  - Color: Off-white #FAF9F6
  - Peso: Regular (no bold)
  - Posición: Centrado, vertical 40% desde arriba

SUBTÍTULO: "Patient Education"
  - Fuente: Sans-serif ligero (Inter Light, Montserrat Light)
  - Tamaño: ~40px
  - Color: Gris claro #A0B4A8
  - Posición: Centrado, debajo de la línea decorativa, 8px de espacio

DETALLE DECORATIVO: Pequeño rombo/diamond ✦ en la esquina inferior derecha
  - Color: #6B8F7A (verde grisáceo)
  - Tamaño: ~24px

FORMATO: 1920×1080, 16:9
```

## USO DE LAS REFERENCIAS EN GOOGLE FLOW

Para cada imagen nueva que generes:
1. Abre la referencia correspondiente como imagen base (image-to-image)
2. Copia el prompt canónico exacto
3. Agrega SOLO los cambios específicos del estado que necesitas
   (ej: "el molar central tiene caries profunda en la superficie oclusal")
4. NO cambies el resto del prompt canónico

Esto garantiza que todas las imágenes de todos los videos sean visualmente consistentes.
